This project has developed an web-based interactive map tool that allows users to zoom in to an area or corridor of interest on the map, specify an optional buffer zone, and obtain a summary report for site-specific impacts related to rail transportaion.

What is a layer?

A "layer" used in the Geographic Information System (GIS) community refers to a data source that defines how spatial data are visually represented on the map. Examples include: railroad (lines), wetlands (polygons), and location (points) of grade crossings.

What data layers are included in this tool?

About 40 layers of environmental, socioeconomic, demographic, and transportation data are currently included in this tool. Data layers are organized in eight folders: Transit Routes, Rail Infrastructure, Community Profile, Safety, Livable Communities, Transit Equity, and Environmental Sustainability, and Potential High Adverse Impact Areas.

Please refer to the Data Annotation table here.

How to view the data layer(s)?

First click on the box next to the sub-folder name, then click on the specific data layer(s). For example, to view the data layer of “Grade Crossing”, check the box next to “Rail Infrastructure” before checking the box next to “Grade Crossing”.

Can I see a regional map that includes all the environmental sensitive areas?

Yes. Check the box next to the folder of “Potential High Adverse Impact Areas” – it includes Critical Habitat, Wetlands, Water, Riparian Zone, Aquifers, Areas of High Accident Potential, and areas of High Risks of Flood Hazard.

How to specify an area of interest on the map?

In Step 2 – Draw an Area of Interest, you have multiple choices - a point, multiple points, a straight line, a freehand line, polygon, or freehand polygon. Click on the button of the shape that you choose and hover mouse over the map. You may also find instructions in a prompt text box.

Which browser should I use?

We recommend using Firefox or Google Chrome for interactive map tool. Depending on the size of specific data layer(s) and internet connection speed, you may experience slow responses at times.
