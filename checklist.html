The Scorecard is structured according to the type of rail project and environmental impact.

Users may select one Project Type:

  • New Construction
  • Renovation
  • Emergency Management
  • Operations

    • Under each Project Type, there are multiple Project Phases:

  • Planning
  • Design
  • Construction
  • Operations & Maintenance

    • Under each Project Phase, there are a number of Goals. Each Goal is linked to a Performance Standard, describing the desired environmental outcome and an associated quantitative metric. Goals are also linked to Benchmarks. Where data is available, the Benchmark is a national average or regional comparison. Where data is not available, the Benchmark is based on a best practice standard.

    The Goals are categorized as Tier 1 or Tier 2. Tier 1 Goals meet minimum regulatory requirements or very commonly used assessment criteria. Tier 2 Goals are not commonly evaluated in Illinois, but are recommended as a "best practice” sustainable alternative. The tiered approach allows the user to choose sustainability metrics that meet the requirements of their evaluation process.

    The Checklist distinguishes between Spatial and Non-Spatial Goals. Spatial Goals are visualized on the tool’s interactive map. Non-Spatial Goals are captured on the Sustainability Checklist, but not represented on the GIS map.
